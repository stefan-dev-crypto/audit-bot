Summary:
The StablecoinMinter contract suffers from a critical vulnerability due to an unsafe calculation of DECIMAL_ADJUSTMENT in the constructor, which can result in an underflow and immediate revert if aidDecimals < stablecoinDecimals. The mint functionality allows anyone to mint AID tokens without constraint, solely backed by transfer of stablecoins, without any form of peg enforcement or price oracle checks, critically undermining the security and economic assumptions. Additionally, the use of a low-level boolean return pattern for ERC20 transfer calls makes the contract incompatible with many well-known ERC20 implementations (e.g., USDT). The combine of sweep function with onlyOwner modifier, if exploited by ownership hijacking, allows draining user funds, and the contract contains interface/inheritance errors that would prevent compilation.
