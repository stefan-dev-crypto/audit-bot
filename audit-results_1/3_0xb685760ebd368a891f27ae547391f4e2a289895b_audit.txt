Summary:
The contract implements token and ETH swap and withdrawal functionalities. It is protected by a ReentrancyGuard, but lacks fundamental authorization and access control on critical swap and withdrawal processes. There is no logic to complete any actual token swap/bridging; assets are only transferred into the contract, and only the owner can withdraw them arbitrarily. As written, the contract effectively acts as a honeypot or a black hole for deposited funds. Critical issues include loss of user funds and potential exit scam vector.


Critical Issues Found:

1. User Funds Are Locked and Only Withdrawable by Owner
   Type: Missing Swap/Bridging Logic; Owner-Only Withdraw
   Function: swap,swapEth,withdraw,withdrawETH
   
   Attack Scenario:
   Users deposit their tokens or ETH to the contract using swap or swapEth. There is no mechanism for the users to reclaim their funds or for the contract to send funds to any bridging system. The owner can always withdraw all assets using withdraw or withdrawETH.
   
   Impact:
   Critical loss of all user funds: any ETH or tokens sent are locked, and only recoverable by the owner. Users have no recourse.
   
   Recommended Fix:
   undefined

   Type: Malicious Owner Control
   Function: swap,swapEth,withdraw,withdrawETH
   
   Attack Scenario:
   Malicious deployer deploys the contract, markets the bridging functionality, collects user deposits in large quantities, and simply withdraws the assets to their own account.
